Destinations

Museum

The Museum of Manila serves as a repository of the city’s rich historical and cultural heritage. It features a diverse collection of artifacts, artworks, and exhibits that illustrate Manila’s evolution through the centuries. The museum provides visitors with a deeper understanding of the city's past and its significant role in the nation's history.

Luneta Park

Luneta Park, also known as Rizal Park, is one of the most significant landmarks in the Philippines. It serves as a tribute to the national hero, Dr. José Rizal, whose monument stands as a symbol of patriotism. The park’s expansive grounds, gardens, and historical markers provide an ideal setting for reflection, recreation, and appreciation of the nation's history.

Intramuros

Intramuros, known as the "Walled City," is a historical district that embodies Manila’s colonial past. Built during the Spanish era, it features well-preserved structures, including ancient churches, fortifications, and plazas. As a symbol of resilience and heritage, Intramuros offers a unique glimpse into the country’s Spanish-era legacy.
